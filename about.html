About Me

Samyam Subedi — Sydney, NSW

I am a Bachelor of Laws student at the University of Technology Sydney. My academic journey bridges the gap between legal scholarship and hands-on cybersecurity — a combination that positions me for the growing field of technology law, digital policy, and cyber regulation.

"Law and technology aren't separate disciplines — they're a conversation. I'm here to be fluent in both."

I hold myself to a simple standard: if I've been given the opportunity, I owe it my best effort. Anything less feels like a waste of something rare.What drives me, at the core, is curiosity — not the casual kind, but a relentless need to understand how things work, why they are the way they are, and what lies just beyond what I currently know. I've found that the more I learn, the more the world opens up. Knowledge doesn't just satisfy curiosity, it creates opportunity and opportunity is what I'm really after.

My competitive debating and ethics background has refined my ability to construct and critically analyse arguments — skills equally valuable in a courtroom or a policy brief. The ADF Young Innovators Award, awarded twice, reflects my drive to apply analytical thinking beyond the classroom.
